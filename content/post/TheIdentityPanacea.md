As a new father, I desperately looked for ways to put my young one to sleep 
regularly. After weeks of struggle, we came across a surprisingly simple 
solution - the sling wrap. The sling is basically a long piece of cloth 
typically 5 meters long and less than a meter wide, made of stretchy fabric. 
I wrap my son in it (like in the left picture above) and he goes to sleep in a 
few minutes usually. The dimensions of the sling wrap, now popular throughout 
the western world is so close to our very own saree, our traditional wear also 
famous for its multi-utility, when it struck me that the sling wrap is perhaps 
an Indian invention, now reapppearing in Europe. 
Such discoveries of India's contributions usually makes me proud of my Indian 
identity. Just as I was about to cement that pride, a bolt of revelation 
struck me.  

The baby sling is an invention much older than I thought. Perhaps older than 
the invention of the wheel itself. Timothy Taylor, an 
archeologist from the University of Bradford, in his book "The Artificial Ape", 
speculates the invention of slings to about two million year ago! 
Mind you, humans as we know them--Homo Sapiens--have been around for about 
200,000 years. The technology to cut and hunt have been dated to abut 50,000 
years ago. This means that the sling is not a Homo Sapien invention, it is 
perhaps a proto-human invention. 
In fact the sling helped take gestation of babies out of the womb, enabling 
us to have bigger brains three times the size of our closest ape alive, 
the chimpanzee. To me, it is convincing because there must have been a genetic
mutation that lead to premature birth of human babies making them 
incapable of locomotion soon as they were born. 
This mutation perhaps lead to the first artificial selection. Artificial 
selection is what has lead to the creation of thousands of breeds of dogs in the 
last couple of centuries, simply because human being chose them for a particular
feature and ensured their further breeding. The genius of the sling made us 
mentally stronger allowing initial physical incapabilities. I want you to take 
this with a pinch of salt, just as I do. All this is just a possibility.

The possibility that humans invented millions of years ago, the kind of 
invention that makes me proud suggests that our inventivenes existed before
identities began taking shape. Identity has been a central force in our history.
Identification through religion, political borders, occupation and skin color 
takes prime time in our television debates today. What we have achieved, we 
ascribe to our upbringing, tied to our identity of some form. For most of us,
identity is not even a possibility of genetic similarity. It is a superficial 
one, like being black and white. 

This possibility 
put me to shame. Any human being with decent perception could have invented the sling. Anyone could have discovered the number zero, law of gravitation, the number system or even the planetary motion.
